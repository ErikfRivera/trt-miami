## What goes into the cost of TRT in Miami

A defensible TRT program — one that aligns with Endocrine Society and AUA guidance [1][2] — has five cost components. Telehealth subscription clinics often advertise a low monthly number by quietly omitting one or more of them.

### Initial consult and physical

An initial visit covers your symptom history, exam, and a discussion of treatment options. Across U.S. TRT clinics, an initial consult bundled with baseline labs typically runs **$50–$300**, with some comprehensive workups priced at **$500 or more**. A proper consult is not a five-minute telehealth intake — it sets the baseline that every dose decision is judged against for years.

### Baseline lab panel (itemized)

The industry-standard baseline panel for cash-pay TRT clinics includes total testosterone, free testosterone, SHBG, estradiol, hematocrit, PSA, LH/FSH, CBC, CMP, and a lipid panel. These labs are required to confirm a hypogonadism diagnosis (FDA-approved indication), establish a starting dose, and screen for the contraindications that the AUA explicitly calls out — elevated PSA, polycythemia, untreated severe sleep apnea [2]. If a clinic skips this panel, the savings come out of your monitoring, not their margin.

### Medication (injection, pellet, gel)

Medication is the most variable line item and the one most exposed to retail pharmacy pricing [4]:

- **Testosterone cypionate (injection):** Generic, typically the lowest medication cost.
- **Pellets (Testopel® or compounded):** Billed per insertion, every 3–6 months.
- **Topical gels (AndroGel® 1.62%, Testim®, Fortesta®, Vogelxo®, Natesto®):** Brand-name pricing, usually the highest monthly medication cost.
- **Compounded testosterone cream:** Sourced from FDA-registered 503B outsourcing facilities, generally the lowest topical option.

### Follow-up visits and labs

Both the Endocrine Society and AUA require recheck labs after initiation — typically at 6 weeks (during titration), 3–6 months, and then every 6–12 months [1][2]. These visits cover hematocrit (AUA flags dose reduction at >50% and withhold at ≥54%) [2], PSA trend, estradiol, and serum testosterone measured midway between injections. Programs that bundle follow-up labs into a flat monthly fee tend to look more expensive on paper than telehealth subscriptions — and considerably cheaper once you add up the à la carte lab bills.

### Ancillary medications (estradiol management, etc.)

A subset of patients need anastrozole for elevated estradiol or other supportive medications. These are prescribed only when labs justify them, not as a default add-on, and they are a small fraction of total program cost when used appropriately.

## TRT injection cost in Miami

Injection programs are typically the lowest-cost modality on a monthly basis. Across U.S. TRT clinics, an all-in injection program — medication, monitoring labs, and clinician visits — runs **$150–$400 per month**. The medication itself (generic testosterone cypionate) is inexpensive at retail [4]; most of the cost reflects the monitoring program, not the vial. Standard cypionate dosing is typically 100–200 mg per week, titrated to a mid-normal serum testosterone of roughly 400–700 ng/dL [1]. Full details on protocols and what to expect are on our [TRT injections page](/trt-injections/).

## TRT pellet cost in Miami

Pellets are priced per insertion rather than monthly. Industry-typical cash-pay pricing runs **$650–$1,500 per insertion**, with **annual costs of roughly $800–$3,500** depending on reinsertion cadence (every 3–6 months) and pellet count. Testopel® is FDA-approved at 75 mg per pellet with a usual dose of 2–6 pellets in men; cash-pay clinics that use compounded pellets often dose 4–12 pellets per insertion. We describe both Testopel® and compounded options — sourced from FDA-registered 503B outsourcing facilities — on the [TRT pellets page](/trt-pellets/).

## TRT gel cost in Miami

Brand-name topical gels are typically the most expensive modality. Industry-typical pricing for **brand-name gels runs $200–$1,000 per month**; **compounded testosterone cream runs $50–$150 per month**. Common options include AndroGel® 1.62%, Testim®, Fortesta®, Vogelxo®, Natesto® (nasal gel), and compounded testosterone cream. Your prescribing clinician will recommend a formulation based on your labs, lifestyle, transfer-risk considerations (children and partners in the household), and insurance coverage. ## Self-pay vs insurance

Most major insurers will cover medically necessary TRT when total testosterone is documented below the diagnostic threshold (typically <300 ng/dL on two early-morning draws) and clinical symptoms of hypogonadism are present. In practice, coverage, prior-authorization rules, and formulary tier vary widely between payers and plans, which is why many TRT programs — including ours — operate on a transparent cash-pay model and provide a detailed superbill you can submit for potential out-of-network reimbursement. **TRT is HSA- and FSA-eligible with a valid prescription.** For plan-specific guidance, see [does insurance cover TRT](/does-insurance-cover-trt/).

## Telehealth TRT vs in-person — what the price difference reflects

Telehealth subscription clinics quote $99–$200 per month, and the gap between that number and an in-person, fully monitored program is real but usually misunderstood. The price difference reflects what is excluded: in-person evaluation, comprehensive baseline labs, scheduled hematocrit and PSA monitoring, and a local clinician who can act on an abnormal result. The Endocrine Society and AUA both expect periodic, hands-on monitoring — not just a refill workflow [1][2]. If telehealth is the right fit clinically, that is a clinical decision. It should not be a price decision.

## Red flags — when TRT is priced too low to be safe

A handful of pricing signals usually indicate skipped monitoring rather than operational efficiency:

- **No baseline labs**, or a panel limited to total testosterone only.
- **No hematocrit monitoring**, despite AUA guidance to investigate at >50% and withhold at ≥54% [2].
- **No PSA baseline** in men ≥40, or no plan for recheck at 3–12 months [1].
- **No in-person option** for the first visit or for follow-ups when labs trend abnormal.
- **No scheduled recheck cadence** — refills issued without labs.

If you are price-shopping, ask each clinic what is included in the monthly figure and what is billed separately. See [is TRT safe](/is-trt-safe/) for the full monitoring rationale.

## FAQ

**How much does TRT cost per month in Miami?**
Self-pay TRT programs in Miami typically range from **$150–$400 per month** for injection-based programs (all-in: medication, visits, and labs), with pellets billed per insertion and brand-name gels running higher. Telehealth subscription clinics quote $99–$200 per month but often exclude labs and lack the in-person monitoring required by AUA and Endocrine Society guidelines [1][2]. Please [contact us for current Strong Health pricing](/contact/).

**Is TRT cheaper with injections, pellets, or gels?**
Generally, generic testosterone cypionate injections are the lowest-medication-cost modality. Pellets are billed per insertion (industry-typical $650–$1,500 every 3–6 months). Brand-name gels are typically the highest monthly medication cost; compounded cream is lower. The right modality is a clinical decision, not a price decision.

**How much do TRT labs cost?**
A baseline TRT panel (total T, free T, SHBG, estradiol, hematocrit, PSA, LH/FSH, lipid, CMP, CBC) runs roughly $50–$300 self-pay when bundled with the initial consult, and more when ordered separately. Most Strong Health programs bundle labs into the program fee.

**Does insurance cover TRT cost in Miami?**
For confirmed hypogonadism, most commercial insurance and Medicare cover medication and lab costs after diagnosis, subject to prior authorization (typically documented total T <300 ng/dL on two early-morning draws plus symptoms) [5]. See [does insurance cover TRT](/does-insurance-cover-trt/) for plan-specific guidance.

**Are cheap online TRT clinics safe?**
Lower-priced telehealth programs that skip in-person evaluation, baseline labs, or hematocrit monitoring fall short of AUA and Endocrine Society standards [1][2]. The cost difference reflects skipped monitoring — not better operations.

**How long am I committed financially?**
TRT is generally a long-term therapy. You can stop at any time, but symptoms typically return as endogenous production has been suppressed. Plan for at least a 12-month horizon.
