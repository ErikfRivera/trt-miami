# Testosterone Pellet Therapy in Miami

Testosterone pellets are small, bioidentical-testosterone implants placed under the skin of the hip during a 5-minute in-office procedure at our Miami clinic. Each pellet releases a steady dose of testosterone for 3 to 6 months, eliminating the weekly injection schedule used in standard TRT. At Strong Health we use FDA-approved Testopel as well as compounded bioidentical pellets, with the choice driven by your labs (total T, free T, SHBG, estradiol, hematocrit) and your treatment goals. This page explains who is a candidate, what the procedure involves, costs, and how pellets compare to TRT injections and gels.

## What are testosterone pellets?

Testosterone pellets are crystalline, cylindrical implants — roughly the size of a grain of rice — that are placed in the subcutaneous fat of the upper buttock or hip. Once seated, the pellets dissolve slowly and release testosterone directly into the bloodstream at a near-constant rate, producing steady serum levels rather than the peak-and-trough pattern seen with weekly injections [1][2]. Pellet therapy has been a recognized route of testosterone replacement for more than fifty years and is one of the delivery methods discussed in the Endocrine Society Clinical Practice Guideline on testosterone therapy for men with hypogonadism [3].

### Bioidentical testosterone vs synthetic

Both Testopel and the compounded pellets used at Miami TRT clinics deliver bioidentical testosterone — meaning the molecule implanted is chemically identical to the testosterone the human body produces. "Bioidentical" refers to molecular structure, not regulatory status. Testopel is the FDA-approved finished product; compounded pellets are prepared by 503A or 503B pharmacies and are not FDA-approved as finished drug products, even though the active ingredient (testosterone USP) is.

### Testopel vs compounded (Biote/SottoPelle) protocols

Strong Health offers two pellet pathways. The first is **Testopel®**, the FDA-approved testosterone pellet. Each Testopel pellet contains 75 mg of testosterone, and typical adult-male dosing is 2 to 6 pellets every 3 to 6 months, depending on labs and clinical response [5]. The second pathway is **compounded bioidentical pellets**, used by clinic protocols often branded as Biote or SottoPelle. We source compounded pellets from FDA-registered 503B outsourcing facilities. Compounded cash-pay regimens are typically larger — in the range of 4 to 12 pellets per insertion, with reinsertion every 3 to 4 months — reflecting both higher per-pellet dosing flexibility and different release kinetics. Your prescribing clinician will discuss which option fits your labs, lifestyle, and budget; cost differences are covered in the [cost of TRT](/trt-cost/) breakdown.

## How the in-office procedure works

Pellet placement is a sterile, scalpel-free procedure performed in a treatment room. From check-in to discharge, most patients are in the clinic for 30 to 45 minutes; the implant itself takes about 5 minutes.

### Pre-procedure labs and consult

Before any pellets are ordered, a comprehensive baseline workup is standard at SERP-leading TRT clinics: total testosterone, free testosterone, SHBG, estradiol, hematocrit, PSA, LH/FSH, CBC, CMP, and a lipid panel. Two early-morning total-testosterone draws below 300 ng/dL plus documented symptoms of hypogonadism are the AUA-aligned diagnostic standard [4]. Strong Health follows this two-draw confirmation pattern before recommending any TRT modality, consistent with our broader approach to [TRT therapy in Miami](/trt-clinic-miami/) and our [HRT Miami](/hrt-miami/) services.

### Step-by-step (numbing, incision, trocar placement, closure)

On procedure day, the upper outer buttock (gluteal fat pad) is cleaned, draped, and infiltrated with local lidocaine. A 3–4 mm incision is made, and a single-use sterile trocar is used to deliver the pellets through a narrow subcutaneous tunnel. The pellets are placed in a fan pattern so they dissolve independently. The trocar is withdrawn, the small opening is closed with Steri-Strips (no stitches in most cases), and a pressure dressing is applied. Most patients describe only pressure during placement and mild soreness for the first 24 to 48 hours.

### Recovery and activity restrictions (48–72 hours)

The dressing stays on for 24 to 48 hours and the site is kept dry for roughly 72 hours. Walking and desk work are fine the same day. Heavy lower-body lifting, swimming, hot tubs, and saunas are typically held for 5 to 7 days to reduce the risk of pellet extrusion and infection at the insertion site. Your prescribing clinician will give you a written aftercare sheet.

## Who is a candidate for pellets in Miami

### Confirmed hypogonadism (two AM total T <300 ng/dL per AUA)

TRT is FDA-approved for men with diagnosed hypogonadism — not for general "low T" lifestyle complaints. The AUA Guideline on Testosterone Deficiency recommends a diagnosis based on two morning total-testosterone draws below 300 ng/dL combined with characteristic symptoms (low libido, fatigue, loss of morning erections, depressed mood, loss of lean mass) [4]. Pellets are an accepted route of therapy in the Endocrine Society guideline once that diagnosis is established [3].

### When pellets are preferred over injections

Patients who travel frequently, dislike self-injection, want the steadiest possible serum levels, or have struggled with weekly-injection adherence often prefer pellets. The set-and-forget cadence — one in-office visit every 3 to 6 months — appeals to busy professionals across Miami-Dade who do not want a weekly home routine. For the broader decision framework across delivery routes, see our overview of [Miami TRT therapy](/trt-clinic-miami/).

### Who should not get pellets

Pellets are generally avoided in men with active or untreated prostate cancer, untreated breast cancer, uncontrolled polycythemia (hematocrit above 54%), severe untreated sleep apnea, severe lower-urinary-tract symptoms, or active desire for near-term fertility. Because pellets cannot be removed easily once implanted, men who are still titrating to a stable dose are often started on injections first. See the broader [is TRT safe?](/is-trt-safe/) discussion for context.

## How long pellets last and re-dosing schedule

### Typical 3–6 month duration; signs you need re-implantation

Testopel typically lasts 3 to 6 months in adult men, depending on metabolism, body composition, activity level, and the number of pellets implanted [1][2][5]. Compounded cash-pay pellet protocols are usually dosed in the 4–12 pellet range and reinserted every 3 to 4 months. Common late-cycle signals — return of fatigue, lower libido, mood drop, reduced workout recovery — are confirmed with a serum total testosterone draw rather than treated by symptoms alone. Strong Health re-checks labs at week 6 to confirm steady-state levels and again before re-implantation.

## Side effects and risks

### Procedure-related (bruising, extrusion, infection)

The most common procedure-related events are bruising at the insertion site and short-lived soreness. Pellet extrusion — where a pellet works its way back out through the incision — occurs in a small percentage of placements in published series and is minimized by proper insertion depth and adherence to 48–72 hour activity restrictions [1]. Infection at the insertion site is uncommon with sterile technique. See [TRT side effects](/trt-side-effects/) for the full safety overview.

### TRT-class (polycythemia, estradiol shift, fertility suppression)

Like all testosterone therapy, pellets can raise hematocrit (polycythemia), shift estradiol upward via aromatization, and suppress endogenous testosterone production and fertility. Because pellets cannot be dose-adjusted mid-cycle, supratherapeutic peaks in the first 4 to 6 weeks after a generous implant are a known consideration, which is why labs are rechecked at week 6.

## Monitoring labs

Strong Health follows guideline-aligned monitoring [3][4]. Labs are typically drawn at week 6 after the first implant, then before each subsequent insertion, with annual checks once stable. The standard recheck panel includes total testosterone, free testosterone, estradiol, hematocrit, and PSA (in men 40+ or with elevated baseline). The AUA threshold for investigating polycythemia is a hematocrit above 50%, with dose reduction or temporary discontinuation at 54% or higher [4]. PSA is rechecked at 3 to 12 months on therapy; urology referral is appropriate if PSA rises more than 1.4 ng/mL in 12 months or exceeds 4.0 ng/mL.

## FAQ

**How long do testosterone pellets last?**
FDA-approved Testopel pellets typically last 3 to 6 months depending on metabolism, activity level, and the number of pellets implanted. Strong Health re-checks labs at week 6 and again before re-implantation.

**How quickly do testosterone pellets work?**
Many patients feel improvement within 7–14 days as testosterone levels rise; full effect on energy, libido, and body composition is typically reached by week 4–6 (per Cleveland Clinic / NIH PMC4431706) [1][2].

**Is the testosterone pellet procedure painful?**
The site is numbed with local lidocaine and the insertion through a 3–4 mm incision takes 3–5 minutes. Most patients report only pressure during placement and mild soreness for 24–48 hours.

**Can testosterone pellets come out?**
Yes — pellet extrusion happens in a small percentage of placements (single-digit % in published series). Proper insertion depth, post-procedure activity restrictions, and avoiding aggressive workouts for 72 hours minimize the risk [1].

**What is the cost of testosterone pellets in Miami?**
See our [TRT cost breakdown](/trt-cost/). Self-pay pellet protocols in Miami typically run $650–$1,500 per insertion (every 3–6 months); insurance coverage varies — see [does insurance cover TRT](/does-insurance-cover-trt/).

**Pellets vs injections — which is better?**
It depends on lifestyle and lab response. Pellets give steady kinetics and no weekly injection; injections allow rapid dose adjustment. Strong Health clinicians review both with you during the initial consult.
